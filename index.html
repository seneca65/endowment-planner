Instead of withdrawing a fixed percentage each year, the Yale formula blends two numbers to keep your income stable through market swings:

70% × (last year's withdrawal × inflation factor) + 30% × (withdrawal rate × current portfolio) = recommended withdrawal

The 70% side anchors your income to last year so it stays stable even if the market drops. The 30% side adjusts with the current portfolio value so withdrawals don't grow out of proportion to your balance over time. The Calculate screen shows both sides so you can see exactly how the number was reached.

The guard rails protect your portfolio during market downturns by adjusting your withdrawal based on how far the portfolio has dropped from its high water mark.

Green zone — portfolio is within 20% of its high water mark. Take the full Yale smoothed withdrawal as normal.
Yellow zone — portfolio has dropped 20–30% from its high water mark. Freeze withdrawals at last year's amount adjusted for inflation only. No increase this year.
Red zone — portfolio has dropped more than 30% from its high water mark. Stop all portfolio withdrawals entirely. Use your emergency fund for living expenses and let the portfolio recover undisturbed.

The 20% and 30% thresholds are adjustable in Settings to match your personal risk tolerance.

The high water mark (HWM) is the highest portfolio value you have ever recorded. It is your reference point for measuring how far the portfolio has fallen from its peak.

You enter the HWM once in Settings and it is saved and carried forward automatically. Update it whenever your portfolio reaches a new all-time high — that new high becomes the new HWM going forward.

Keeping the HWM accurate is important because the guard rail zones are calculated as a percentage drop from the HWM. An outdated HWM will give you incorrect zone readings.

The prior year withdrawal is the amount you withdrew last year. It is the anchor for the 70% side of the Yale formula — this year's recommendation is partly based on last year's amount adjusted for inflation.

You only need to enter it manually once — your very first year. After that, every time you tap Record, the app automatically saves the withdrawal amount and uses it as next year's prior year figure. You never have to update it manually again.

If you ever need to correct it, you can edit it directly in Settings.

The red zone assumes you have a separate emergency fund — money held outside your investment portfolio in cash, a money market account, or short-term bonds — that you can draw from during a severe market downturn without selling investments at a loss.

A typical guideline is to keep 1–2 years of living expenses in your emergency fund. This gives your portfolio time to recover from a 30%+ drop without forcing you to sell at the bottom.

When the portfolio recovers back above the red zone threshold, you resume normal withdrawals from the portfolio and replenish the emergency fund over time.

Go to the History tab and tap Export history to CSV. This downloads a spreadsheet file containing all your recorded years — the year, withdrawal amount, zone, portfolio value, and how far below the high water mark you were.

You can open the CSV file in Excel, Google Sheets, or Numbers to keep a permanent record outside the app.
